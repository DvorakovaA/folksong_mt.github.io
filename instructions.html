Instructions

In this annotation task, we are interested in the translation of the lexical content of folk songs' lyrics.
We are not interested in rhymes, schema, etc. Translation of each word matters to us in the lyrics context to a reasonable extent (see section examples).
To evaluate machine translation capability on folk song lyrics, we present you, annotators, with a number of translated songs to evaluate translation on them.

Steps:

For each word in the original lyrics, decide whether:
  • It is semantically important and is represented in the English translation (Enter)
  • It is semantically important but is incorrectly represented or not represented at all in the English translation (Del/Backspace)
  • It is not important for the meaning (do nothing; just skip it using the arrow keys)

Finally, three overall questions:
  • Rate the song translation with 0 to 5 stars (the more stars, the better the translation in terms of conveying the meaning of the text as a whole).
  • Answer whether you spot some hallucinated (content bearing) words in the English translation, that were not present in any way in the original.
  • Select the level of presence of unusual language in the song (dialect, archaic words, ...).
Optionally, you can add a comment regarding your annotation or the song, etc.

Details

What is a semantically important word? These are words that you consider essential for the content of the given verse.
Typically, these will be autosemantic parts of speech: nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs. You do not have to overthink this.
As a sort of helper, we tried to automatically highlight these four parts of speech in the texts; these suggestions are annotated by violet underscore, but you do not have to follow them.

We do not care about indentation (and its matching).
Also, if you find a mistake/typo in the original, let it be for now; we are interested in the translation.

Don’t hesitate to just use your intuition when the instructions seem ambiguous.
Due to the nature of human languages, there is no way to make instructions that can be directly applied all the time — your judgment as native speakers is the gold standard here.

Keyboard shortcuts

  • ← / → – move the cursor between words
  • Enter – mark the current word as correctly translated
  • Del / ⌫ – mark the current word as wrongly translated/missing in the translation

The word currently under annotation is coloured yellow.
After pressing Enter, the (yellow) word turns green.
After pressing Del/Backspace, the (yellow) word turns red.
If you do not press any specific key, just the right arrow, the word is annotated as "not important".

In case you made a mistake, you can simply navigate yourself back to the word with the wrong/missing annotation by pressing the arrow keys and then by pressing Enter or Del/Backspac you change or remove the annotation (e.g. if a word is marked as green and the Enter is pressed again, the green is deleted and it is now annotated as "not important").

Saving your work

Your work should be saved in the browser - once you open the app again in same browser on same device and you select same language you should see the button "Resume Evaluation" - please test the functionality after starting your annotation so you do not lose your work accidentally.
You can also export the JSON and, when getting back to work, skip songs already exported, then send both (more) with a comment.

Exporting results

Once you annotate all the songs for the chosen language, a page with a big green button saying "Export Results as JSON".
Please click on it.
It should start a download of a file named folksong_mt_{language}.json (e.g. folksong_mt_slovak.json).
Now, please send that downloaded file to anna.dvorakova332@student.cuni.cz.
Thank you!

Examples

Czech original: Má zlatá Ančičko
(a guy is talking to his beloved girl named Ančička)
Literally translated: My golden Ančička
Also very fine translations: My dear Ančička

Czech original: Z toho Spáňova pudu,
(pudu is a dialect for verb půjdu, or also second case from noun pud meaning instinct)
Literally standard Czech translated: From that Spáňov instinct,
Mistake! And also there appears some dialect in the song.

Again, do not hesitate to use your intuition.
Our goal is to test whether machine translation is already viable for some cross-lingual comparison of this material.
