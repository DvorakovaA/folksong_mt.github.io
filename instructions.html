Instructions

In this annotation task, we are interested in the translation of the overall meaning of folk songs' lyrics.

For each word in the original lyrics, decide whether:
  • It is semantically important and is represented in the English translation (Enter)
  • It is semantically important but is incorrectly represented or not represented at all in the English translation (Del/Backspace)
  • It is not important for the meaning (do nothing; just skip it using the arrow keys)

Finally, rate the song translation with 0 to 5 stars (the more stars, the better the translation in terms of conveying the meaning of the text as a whole).

What is a semantically important word? These are words that you consider essential for the content of the given verse.
Typically, these will be autosemantic parts of speech: nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs. You do not have to overthink this.

For Don’t hesitate to just use your intuition when the instructions seem ambiguous.
Due to the nature of human languages, there is no way to make instructions that can be directly applied all the time — your judgment as native speakers (familiar with folksongs) is the gold standard here.

Keyboard shortcuts

  • ← / → – move the cursor between words
  • Enter – mark the current word as correctly translated
  • Del / ⌫ – mark the current word as wrongly translated/missing in the translation
The word currently under annotation is coloured yellow.
After pressing Enter, the (yellow) word turns green.
After pressing Del/Backspace, the (yellow) word turns red.
If you do not press any specific key, just the right arrow, the word is annotated as "not important".

In case you made a mistake, you can simply navigate yourself back to the word with the wrong/missing annotation by pressing the arrow keys and then by pressing Enter or Del/Backspac you change or remove the annotation (e.g. if a word is marked as green and the Enter is pressed again, the green is deleted and it is now annotated as "not important").
